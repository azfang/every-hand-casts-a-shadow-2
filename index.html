a figure in motion:

I have a fear, i’ve realized,

of stillness.

I am compelled

toward action

even in the slight gestures of my body;

there’s a desire

(in some ways, a compulsion)

to find

myself in perpetual direction.

I am drawn to

the thought of trailing one’s

finger up and down a screen,

the cadence of its rise

and fall, rise and fall.

each morning i perform these

gentle choreographies, imitating

and simulating, always posing,

never quite getting these gestures

right. i paint a living hand

raised in error, its rise and fall—

a figure in motion: the cursor

dances for me — it pirouettes

from point to point,

each line unfolding,

collapsing in on itself.

I lack the ease to move

freely, gracefully, with my own limbs,

though I suppose

I've had an entire lifetime

to gesticulate

to learn to perform

its myriad convulsions, frantic

and frenetic.

a figure in motion:

my mother's father

was the one who taught me

the steadiness of a singular stroke

how to produce it tenderly.

my mother's mother

taught me

to memorize lines

of poetry,

my fingers clasped

behind my back

lest they tremble,

give me away.

A figure in motion:

I’ve left marks

Upon the page,

The surface

you've rendered

as wildly

incomprehensible.

there is some sort of dialogue,

i imagine, trails

i must make, rubbed raw —

desire paths

leaving impressions on the skin —

a figure in motion: the hand

grasps mine

to trace the outlines

of some imagined

past, one that had

bound you

and i, a line

severed years ago.

i lack the ease

as i did back then,

the body

the hand as one

an entire lifetime

to learn to forget

its motions. slow

and steady
